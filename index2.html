About the Author

For the majority of my life I've been told I talk too much and
I've always been under the impression that it was a bad thing.
Now I've realized that my words are my power source.
They are my innermost paramount and ponderous feelings that come through from my heart, to my pen, to you.
And that, my loves, is not bad at all.

I am a blossoming young woman from a small town in Ontario but I assure you this life I live is not small.
This heart I love with is not small.
These dreams I have are far from small.
I was raised in a vibrant Italian world full of loud voices and expression and I've spent my life with my bare feet to the earth learning to quiet that exterior voice and find my interior dialogue.

Writing for me has always been my only outlet.
I've journaled for as long as I can remember and started to develop quite the vocabulary and knack for finding a way to pen-to-paper my feelings about the bewitching world surrounding me.
It was in 2018, whilst living in Tulum, Mexico; surrounded by prodigious musicians and astounding artists who pushed me to take my silent craft and turn it into a publishable art that I could use for work and pleasure.
It was then that I cultivated my first stand-point creative writing/slam poetry piece which would later be used in multiple collaborations with international DJs and win me 1st place in Vancouver's slam poetry finals.

I realized from the appreciation of my loved ones and the glimmer in the eyes of my listeners that this was something that I was meant to do. And so, I do.
And I will continue to do.
For myself. And for you.

Write with light,
Ray

Luna's Lullaby Cry

Last night,
I had a conversation with the moon.
She whispered of sacred dances,
the glimmering midnight breezes strewn.
She fondled with this persona of love,
looking down on her sister Earth,
basking her in silvery light,
guiding the tides to kiss the shores
but never hold their grasp too tight.

We moved together, her and I,
calling upon feelings existent from long ago.
Days where the trifles done with the setting sun
and worry washes in the coming snow.
Her eclipse touched my ear.
Knowledge was gained through the mutters of her silent
shimmering
tears.

She cried over the ripping of plants,
smashing of roots,
sacred medicines torn from the ground
crushed under colonial boots.
Tears in the form of crystallized light beams hit my shoulders,
mimicking an eruption of one thousand fallen soldiers.
With every death we’ve passed on
the information
of how to overcome it.

SHE
She has witnessed it all.
The unfolding of time.
Watching us from above.
Waiting
watching
waiting
waiting
until we evolved from out of pantomime.
And as we did,
she did too.
Learning our language,
Us and Her
Son vs Moon.

Combined
 intertwined
subtle shine
 deafening glow.
That setting ball of fire,
that phantom Northern snow.
And here I was, an inferior beast.
This burden to bear was too much
from this admirable feat.

And so I cried along.

Thinking that the tears would disguise
the
shame
and
betrayal
that fell to place before my eyes.
From my people,
from our movements,
from our disrespect,
from this species
and the hatred
and the disconnected neglect.
But putting things into retrospect,
I sought out the hope of some future effect
Where Love and Earth and Moon and Me
become one.
Become us.
Become he, she, we.

And so,

I let my tears fall to waves
that crashed upon the shore at my feet.
And as my footprints dissolved within my own self,

so did my speech.

Eternal

The first time that they met,
they were two light entities
whose intersonic sources felt heated by another.
Their beams bounced gracefully
across the untouching molecular of each other.
They knew through the holes, which their light contained,
that was filled by that other source,
that they were meant for each other.
Now,
and forever.
And so they developed their future beings with no remorse.

Then their souls touched yet again,
when one was Zeus and one was rain.
And when he touched his sceptre to sky,
her gentle tear fell down again.
And in this connection it truly seemed
that they would never part.
Controlling the frugality of the world
through both connected hearts.
A Galatian wayward soldier
and a maiden in distress.
Both looked into each other’s eyes and saw their
 lengthy
  lifelong
 mess.
She recognized his splendour,
and he her tender heart,
and they knew yet again
that their souls would never part.
Though they couldn’t explain it,
it was undeniable and true,
that when life throws a soul your way,
it could be the eternal half to you.

Lords and ladies gather,
all seem unimpressed.
The gentlemen in their waistcoats,
women in their petty dress.
He brushed up alongside her,
whilst waltzing at the ball.
And felt a surge and utter rush
of their entire rise and fall.
He was blinded by her light,
her beauty,
her unconventional hold over him.
He realized this was one of many times
their lives would sink or swim.
He couldn’t quite ignore it,
how could he? It was fate.
And so he asked her father’s permission to make her his lifemate.

Now he’s a small-town skater,
and she’s a rebel teen,
they meet late at night to stare into each other’s eyes
and relive all they’ve seen.
They may be young, but they both know
the journeys of before.
Even in this technological age,
where love seems such a bore.
But it doesn’t seem trivial to them
because they have old souls.
They were blessed from long ago with an
eternal lover
who truly knows.

Now,
standing tall,
side by side,
in a forest evergreen,
are two ancient oaks with bountiful leaves
whose history of the world they’ve seen.
Their roots are intertwined underneath
the mossy floor of this grass-covered reef,
and their branches reach towards the sky
to praise their old selves who used to fly.
The wind that brushes along their trunks
is a feeling they both share,
because
one
is
two
and
one,
for this there’s no compare.
Though they don’t have brains to think or talk,
their vibrations feel so much.
And these two trees
know
for
certain
that our fingerprints don’t fade
from the lives we touch.

Day One - I AM: intro to the root chakra. LAM
Writing a memory, ancestry, earth/land/home

Day Two - I CREATE: the sacral chakra. VAM
Writing desire, lust description, reaching toward what we creatively desire

Day Three - I BELIEVE: The solar plexus chakra. RAM
The jewel, writing power, power struggles between characters, developing strength and conﬁdence in our personal power

Day Four - the heart chakra. YAM
Writing love, emotions, intimate communication with self

Day Five - I EXPRESS: the throat chakra. HAM
Writing voice and dialogue, developing clarity of voice, overcoming blockages

Day Six - I KNOW: third eye. SHAM
Writing dreams, intuition, fantasy, building and envisioning the bigger picture.

Day Seven - I AM: the crown. OM
