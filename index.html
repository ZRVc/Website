About the Author

For the majority of my life I've been told I talk too much.
I've always been under the impression that it was a bad thing.
What I've realized though, is that my words are my power source.
They are my innermost paramount and ponderous feelings that come through from my heart, to my pen, to you.
And that, my loves, is not bad at all.

I am a blossoming young woman from a small town in Ontario but I assure you this life I live is not small.
This heart I love with is not small.
These dreams I have are far from small.
I was raised in a vibrant Italian world full of loud voices and expression and I've spent my life with my bare feet to the earth learning to quiet that exterior voice and find my interior dialogue.

Writing for me has always been my only outlet.
I've journaled for as long as I can remember and started to develop quite the vocabulary and knack for finding a way to pen-to-paper my feelings about the bewitching world surrounding me.
It was in 2018, whilst living in Tulum, Mexico; surrounded by prodigious musicians and astounding artists who pushed me to take my silent craft and turn it into a publishable art that I could use for work and pleasure.
It was then that I cultivated my first stand-point creative writing/slam poetry piece which would later be used in multiple collaborations with international DJs and win me 1st place in Vancouver's slam poetry finals.

I realized from the appreciation of my loved ones and the glimmer in the eyes of my listeners that this was something that I was meant to do. And so, I do.
And I will continue to do.
For myself. And for you.
Write with light.

Forever and always,
Ray

Circus of Life

Ladies and Gentlemen!
Boys and Girls!
Alpha and Omega!
Welcome.
To the greatest show on Earth!
This one called life.
This heckle and strife.
This universe of stardust,
Earth of majesty,
seat of Mars.
Some other Eden,
just some molecule planted among the stars.

So, all you Adams with your apples
and Eves with your fruitful bearings.
With your proud hearts,
rebellious brows,
deaf ears,
and souls uncaring.
I’m airing this message to you.
It’s time for sharing.

I have a secret.
Can you keep it?
Can you bury it with the roots?
The roots twisting into the ground under your
rotten
fallen
fruits.
Twisting into the core of our very existence,
for one instant
listen.
You
are
MAGIC!
You are a magical being
having a human experience.
Don’t lose your identity!
Every crosscut,
roadblock
and misdirection
is meant to be.

Guided by the prophecies of a higher divination,
a power that overcomes every man-made creation.
But we often have this fixation,
to ease our self-deprivation,
a validation of sorts;
that the world lies in the palms of our hands.
But we’re wrong,
the lines on our palms are only mere things we barely understand.
Rivers flowing through the forests of our souls’ shadowlands.
Revealing our history
through deep grooves of all the things
we’re trying to reprimand.
Seeping into a contradiction of our future.
A future we need to mend!

So use those hands!
To move mountains!
Shape valleys!
Castles of clay!
Empires of sand and glass!
Assemble! Rally!
Get off your ass!

Because our bones only grow to stiffen.
And our hair turns to grey.
We don’t want our footprints
to be left in disarray.
We strive to outlast this life,
to quicken this walk to a run.
Having too many quarrels with God
to accept a salvation too easily won.

So the secret is told.
Did you figure it out?

COMMUNICATION
OF
CONSCIOUSNESS

That’s what this life is about.

Luna's Lullaby Cry

Last night,
I had a conversation with the moon.
She whispered of sacred dances,
the glimmering midnight breezes strewn.
She fondled with this persona of love,
looking down on her sister Earth,
basking her in silvery light,
guiding the tides to kiss the shores
but never hold their grasp too tight.

We moved together, her and I,
calling upon feelings existent from long ago.
Days where the trifles done with the setting sun
and worry washes in the coming snow.
Her eclipse touched my ear.
Knowledge was gained through the mutters of her silent
shimmering
tears.

She cried over the ripping of plants,
smashing of roots,
sacred medicines torn from the ground
crushed under colonial boots.
Tears in the form of crystallized light beams hit my shoulders,
mimicking an eruption of one thousand fallen soldiers.
With every death we’ve passed on
the information
of how to overcome it.

SHE
She has witnessed it all.
The unfolding of time.
Watching us from above.
Waiting
watching
waiting
waiting
until we evolved from out of pantomime.
And as we did,
she did too.
Learning our language,
Us and Her
Son vs Moon.

Combined
 intertwined
subtle shine
 deafening glow.
That setting ball of fire,
that phantom Northern snow.
And here I was, an inferior beast.
This burden to bear was too much
from this admirable feat.

And so I cried along.

Thinking that the tears would disguise
the
shame
and
betrayal
that fell to place before my eyes.
From my people,
from our movements,
from our disrespect,
from this species
and the hatred
and the disconnected neglect.
But putting things into retrospect,
I sought out the hope of some future effect
Where Love and Earth and Moon and Me
become one.
Become us.
Become he, she, we.

And so,

I let my tears fall to waves
that crashed upon the shore at my feet.
And as my footprints dissolved within my own self,

so did my speech.

Five Parts to the Face

1.
A hairline that recedes like the carribean sea
Against a less than milk chocolate forehead shore.
Falling stars of whisping baby hairs, learning to walk
blink and they’re gone,
grown-up.

2.
Eyes far apart enough that
a wire ending in tin cans can pass from window to window
so souls can speak.
Venom brown mixed with blades of grass
that melt into an eclipse all on their own

3.
A nose that slightly hooks at the end
to hang the worm
the early bird flies so fast for.
Just to bait
all your bullshit
that I can sniff out like a bloodhound.

4.
The parted pout of those two lips
open slowly
like two legs of your sleepy mistress when you remind her how much you love her.
And with those same lips,
she mouth the words back.

5.
Her chin is earthquake split down the middle,
which reminds her of her father.
One finger rests in the dimple
to lift her head up to its proper proud height.

Free Spirit Girl

They tell me,
“you’re such a free spirit.”
But
what happens when your spirit is too free?
When it’s too whimsical to hold onto?
When they can’t keep it in their mouth
long enough to taste?
What happens when your
freedom
is your crusted dried rose,
hung up in the windowsill
for petty lovers to remember the blossom you used to be?
WHAT HAPPENS TO THAT FREE?

They tell me,
“you’re such a wild thing.”
But
what happens when they think your wild has been willed over their worth?
When you wild your way through that open range,
and leave them dusted in their dependent path?
What happens to those
horned teeth savannah beasts,
hunted for the things that once brought an appreciated smile?
WHAT HAPPENS TO THAT WILD?

They tell me,
“you’re such an old soul.”
But
what happens when that old is too soulful?
When the dust layered on your record grooves
diminishes on their fingertips?
And your wrinkles show in all their reflections.
What happens to that ancient knowledge
they don’t even know you possess?
Calligraphed on your life’s scroll.
WHAT HAPPENS TO THAT OLD SOUL?

My legs pace forward at a rate of slow comfort
Like breastfeeding from your mother
I am
in no rush, no hurry to life’s finish line
But the Walker bys are runnerbys
To their desks and their streetlights
Waiting in mild patience

to rush their moments
These precious 60 second jewels
Rare and exquisite
Hiding in bland grey stone that only hits you like a boulder
Only when it has become to late
It may be to late for them yet
Rapid rejection for memories they’ll soon forget
Trying to keep up with their frantic minds

almost

1000 Kisses Deep

Life takes its toll, accept defeat.
A laneway love on a walking street.
But here I lay, cheek to cheek,
with you my love, beneath the sheets.
Hoping I can taste you;
lips
to
lips
we meet.
I finally have the pleasure
to be 1000 kisses deep.

You play me like a violin,
you pluck upon my strings.
A Hindi tune, an ethnic feel,
pulse racing to its beat.
A combination of lovers’ songs,
soundtrack on repeat.
Play me LOUD
and play me long,
1000 kisses deep.

I’m just another sentence
on your page that’s incomplete.
A paragraph to graze through,
another discarded sheet.
Oh how I wish
you’d rewrite this,
open your eyes to see.
That you and I
could win the prize,
1000 kisses deep.

I’ve traversed lands with mountain sheep.
Sunrise,
sunset,
a twilight sleep.
The earth I’d course, for pain I’d weep.
Shed 1000 tears for you,
1000 kisses deep.

Ocean waves are rolling,
tumbled rock and melted sleet.
Crashing up upon the shore
to lovers’ sandy feet.
A gentle breeze of quick dismay,
a melted cone of freeze.
Beneath the grain the lovers lay,
1000 kisses deep.

I think you’re everlasting
in days where beauty is obsolete.
Your look so elegant, so dashing,
true grace that you excrete.
There’s something of your laughter
that makes me feel complete.
A face to sail 1000 ships,
1000 kisses deep.

The kisses that I’d kiss you,
a lick upon your teeth.
A mile and I’d miss you,
a heavy
trodden
street.
Somehow though, I feel I’m with you.
Somewhere we shall meet.
Here
or in the ever after,
1000 kisses deep.
